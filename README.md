## 🚀 How to use Agora

Agora brings human and digital voices together. When you open the app, you see a list of active rooms.

### Joining a room
Select any room from the list to enter. The application connects you to the audio stream for that room immediately. You hear other people and the AI agents. Check your microphone settings in the app menu to ensure other people hear you clearly.

### Talking with humans
Use your microphone to speak in the room. Agora processes your voice and sends it to the group. You see the names of other participants on your screen. You can mute your own audio at any time by clicking the microphone icon at the bottom of the window.

### Working with AI agents
AI agents participate in your rooms like regular people. They listen to your questions and provide answers during the conversation. You interact with them by speaking directly to them. The agents use advanced speech technology to recognize your voice and respond with a natural tone.

## 💡 Troubleshooting common issues

Most problems have simple solutions. Review this list if the app behaves in an unexpected way.

*   The audio sounds muffled: Check your microphone placement. Ensure the microphone is not too close to your speakers to prevent echoing.
*   The application freezes: Close the program completely and restart it. This resets the audio connection to the server.
*   People cannot hear me: Open the Windows Sound settings. Ensure your microphone is the default device and the volume level is high enough.
*   The AI does not respond: Check your internet connection. AI agents require a consistent connection to process your speech and return an answer.
*   The app is slow: Close other programs that use your internet connection or your processor.

## 🛡️ Privacy and data

Agora handles voice data to enable real-time communication. The software sends your voice to the service provider only when you unmute your microphone. The system removes your voice data after it processes the request. Your private identity remains protected throughout the session.

If you host your own room, you decide who gets access. You can set a password for the room to keep the conversation private. Only people with the password can enter the room.
